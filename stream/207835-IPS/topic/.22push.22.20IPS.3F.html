Topic: "push" IPS?

Isaac Vetter (Feb 09 2022 at 20:15):

I understand that we're in the process of specifying how IPS documents can be queried ($docref, $summary). Has the group discussed how IPS documents should be pushed?

Isaac Vetter (Feb 09 2022 at 20:15):

It seems like a simple POST /Bundlewould work, right?

  • http://www.hl7.org/fhir/documents.html#bundle

Vassil Peytchev (Feb 09 2022 at 20:34):

Note that POST /Bundle implies that the Bundle is persisted as is, and available to subsequent read and search interactions. I am not sure that there are many servers ready or willing to do that.

Rob Hausam (Feb 09 2022 at 20:57):

If the Bundle is persisted intact as a document bundle, I think that HAPI and many other servers shouldn't have any problem with that. What we may need for IPS though, is some kind of "hybrid" behavior on a server, possibly where the document bundle could be persisted (or re-assembled without loss), plus the ability to also break down the bundle into its components that are available for secondary uses (e.g., generating a vaccine certificate or incorporating into the expected locations in an EHR?). That would be custom behavior for IPS, but I don't see in principle why something like that couldn't be done.

Elliot Silver (Feb 09 2022 at 22:15):

Yeah, we've certainly been wondering about the receiving end of IPS. Lots of work going on with generating them and getting them into a patient's hands, but little discussion about what to do to get it back into a clinician's system. Do we expect the documents to be persisted as received, or document patient resources replaced with system patient resources, etc. We don't have an answer for any of this yet.

Peter Jordan (Feb 09 2022 at 23:51):

Vassil Peytchev said:

Note that POST /Bundle implies that the Bundle is persisted as is, and available to subsequent read and search interactions. I am not sure that there are many servers ready or willing to do that.

You can do that on my Server (Terminz) subject to the following...
Bundle.Type == Bundle.BundleType.Document
Composition.Type.coding.system="http://loinc.org" and code="60591-5"
Contains one Composition and one Patient Resource
Contains at least one of each of these resources - AllergyIntolerance, Condition and MedicationStatement (or MedicationRequest).
Includes at least one Patient.Identifier with populated system and value elements (these are used for persistence and $summary request purposes)
Requests are via the patient/$summary operation deferred to R6 by PA yesterday.
I don't support requests for the individual resources within the Composition.
