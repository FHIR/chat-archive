Topic: JIRA Ballot: Link Vote

Hans Buitendijk (Apr 30 2021 at 20:53):

Couple of questions:

  • Can I link my vote to multiple people (e.g., within our organization to everybody who entered JIRA votes)? It appears to only allow linking to one. Anything beyond that you would have to go to every JIRA and vote individually.
  • Will votes submitted by the person you linked to AFTER you linked to that person also be included in your vote? Or do you have to wait until the person you linked to entered all of their JIRAs/vote?

Lloyd McKenzie (Apr 30 2021 at 21:54):

You can only link to one. This mirrors the past behavior of "see comments submitted by X". HL7 has never (to my knowledge) supported a "see comments submitted by X and Y" capability. However, the voter you point to could certainly vote on comments submitted by multiple people - and everyone who linked to that voter would be treated as having voted on the same issues.

The process is that once you link, your overall vote automatically changes as the linked-to submission changes. When the ballot closes and the individual submissions are transitioned to 'closed', anyone who voted "same as" will automatically have all of the detailed vote comments of the original voter cloned into their submission. In short - only constraints on timing of establishing the linkage are:

  • must happen after the "linked to" submission has a vote of some kind (simplest is for them to abstain shortly after the ballot opens)
  • must happen before the ballot closes
    Linked submissions will be identical to the submissions they're linked to once the ballot closes
