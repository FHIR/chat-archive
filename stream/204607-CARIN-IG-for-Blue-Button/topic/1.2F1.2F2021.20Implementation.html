Ryan Harrison (Sep 09 2020 at 15:59):

The practical outcome is to, at a minimum, state your version of C4BB compatibility (in C4BB CapabilityStatement).

This will still confuse app developers, who will in all likelihood be expecting the latest release. Moreover, app clients are not required to assert their compatibility to the server. It's very possible to have an app client implicitly assume latest, while the server is using a prior version (as correctly indicated in the server CapabilityStatement).

For future reference, in interim release v0.1.3 (8 September 2020), the version convention is changed to: Release.Ballot.Version
This means that what Howells refered to as v.0.3.1 precedes v0.1.2 (because v0.1.2 uses the updated versioning convention).

Ryan Howells (Sep 15 2020 at 00:37):

Actually, v0.3.1 comes after v0.1.2. The reason why is because we recently needed to update the versioning to conform with HL7's standard. The HL7 version naming convention is: v.b.r
v = published version number. We are trying to get to "1", but are currently pre-publication of 1.0, so we are v = 0
b = ballot number for this version. CARIN IG for BB has been balloted once, so b = 1
r = revision number. The IG team can use this as they wish. This is the third publication of the CI build, so we have used 3.
So, the latest CI build is version to 0.1.3.

You can always find the most updated information about the open JIRA ticket items we have left associated with the IG here: https://confluence.hl7.org/pages/viewpage.action?pageId=91986623 or you can join our weekly public calls which are also located on the same page. We don't have much left. Here are the major items.

Finishing the definitions of Value Sets and Code Systems
Reviewing a few cardinality discrepancies between the IG and the Worksheet
Adding the vision and dental profiles to the worksheet
Final QA and some updates to the background section of the content
