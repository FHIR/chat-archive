Corey Spears (Jul 16 2021 at 22:08):

@Pat Taylor , Is the intent to be able to retrieve the referenced resources after the EoB has been retrieved or to be able to retrieve the EoB and all referenced resources together? The former is handled through GET operations using the references in the EoB, the latter is handled through an _include. As defined right now, the _include is problematic because they are supposed to only be resources that are also search parameters. The resources can be retrieved separately without the use of _include.

Pat Taylor (Jul 19 2021 at 11:43):

@Corey Spears The intent is to allow the app to either retrieve the referenced resources after the EOB has been retrieved or to retrieve the EOB and all reference resources together. However, the reference resources cannot be retrieved without first retrieving the EOB; i.e., they are always retrieved within the context of the EOB

Paul Church (Jul 19 2021 at 15:20):

I don't perceive an actual difference here - I think people are talking past each other.

The IG requires support for 5 particular _includes, as per the capability statement. 4 of them are standard search params (I think insurer isn't? maybe an oversight there?) It does not require the ability to search by these params, but it's not forbidden. You could trigger any of these includes individually.

The IG also requires _include:*. As per the standard, this will trigger every _include that the server supports, which will definitely be at least those 5 and potentially more.
