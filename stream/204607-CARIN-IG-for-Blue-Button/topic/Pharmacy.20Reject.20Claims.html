Jason Teeple (Aug 21 2020 at 19:17):

@Amol Vyas @Pat Taylor Is it the intention of the EOB Pharmacy mapping to represent rejected claims?

Rejected claims in the pharmacy world are different from rejected claims in the Medical world. Medical claims are typically submitted after the service is completed; pharmacy claims are submitted before the medication is dispensed. As a result, if a claim is rejected in the medical world, it represents something that happened to the patient, but the insurer decided not to pay for. On the other hand, if a pharmacy claim is rejected, it represents a dispensing event that did not occur.
