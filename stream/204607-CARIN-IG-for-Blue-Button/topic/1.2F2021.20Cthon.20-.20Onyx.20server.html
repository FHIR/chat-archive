Hi Michele! I think you may be using an older endpoint because we have no "Ruby" entities in play this time around.

Our SAFHIR Portal is here, where you can register yourself as a developer, then create an application registration: https://con.safhir.io

There is one caveat when you get your application credentials... Your 'scope' field is not going to correspond to a correct set of S-o-F scopes. For your testing, simply submit scopes for the corresponding IG you are tracked to (see below):

CarinBB patient access
launch/patient openid fhirUser offline_access patient/ExplanationOfBenefit.read patient/Coverage.read patient/Organization.read patient/Patient.read patient/Practitioner.read

US-Core patient access
launch/patient openid fhirUser offline_access patient/AllergyIntolerance.read patient/CarePlan.read patient/CareTeam.read patient/Condition.read patient/Device.read patient/DiagnosticReport.read patient/DocumentReference.read patient/Encounter.read patient/Goal.read patient/Immunization.read patient/Location.read patient/Medication.read patient/MedicationRequest.read patient/Observation.read patient/Organization.read patient/Patient.read patient/Practitioner.read patient/Procedure.read patient/Provenance.read

NOTE: We do not support limited-access in terms of scope. This means that, if you want to track to either CBB or us-core, you must submit all of the resources as scopes to be verified. See the examples above for a model, you may submit only patient-level or only user-level, but again, you must request all resources for that IG in the scope field if you want either or both.
