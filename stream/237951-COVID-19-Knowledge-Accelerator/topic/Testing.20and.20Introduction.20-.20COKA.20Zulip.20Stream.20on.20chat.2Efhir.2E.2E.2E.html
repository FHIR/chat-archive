Christiaan Knaap (May 27 2020 at 09:04):

That is a deep thought @Grahame Grieve ! @Khalid Shahin : sorry for the delay but here is my (obviously slightly biased) view:
The're both capable FHIR Servers. The most obvious difference is that HAPI is in Java and Vonk is in .NET Core.
Depending on the FHIR features you need, one may have an advantage over the other, but all the common FHIR features are covered by both.
Hapi has Interceptors to add behaviour to the server, Vonk allows for plugins that are dynamically loaded.
Afaik only Vonk has the capability to add custom resource types (and search parameters on them) - even at runtime. That makes it easy to experiment with new and evolving resourcetypes like Citation.
Getting started with Vonk will take you about 5 minutes, instructions are here. And the community edition is free to use.
