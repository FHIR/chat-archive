Topic: Frequency of IG publishing

Diane (Oct 15 2021 at 22:26):

Is there a best practice for frequency of balloting/IG publishing for Questionnaire/SDC IGs? For instance, if every 6 months, we add a few questions to each of our questionnaires, change the order of a few questions and add a new workflow diagram, do we go back for balloting/IG publishing every 6 months? Or can/should we publish a CI build for the IG without balloting?

Lloyd McKenzie (Oct 15 2021 at 22:37):

I think we can safely say that SDC is nowhere close to following any sort of best practice wrt balloting. The ballot closed at the end of April, 2019 and we still haven't gotten the resulting version published yet. I think a reasonable turn-around time is about 2 years, though I actually expect that SDC will go back to ballot in May 2022 with an objective of publishing by end of 2022 to get ballot review on the vast number of things we've introduced since that first ballot. After that, I'd expect another publication about a year or so after that that migrates SDC to R5.

In terms of your IG, as a rule you need to give implementers at least a year before you make any radical changes (so they can implement, provide feedback, you can make the changes, go through ballot, complete resolution and re-publish). Doing all that in one year requires a pretty intense level of effort on everyone's part. If you're just tweaking things, you can also look at using the STU update process. That means going through peer review rather than balloting. Your description of planned changes would definitely fall within the scope of what's allowed by an STU update.
