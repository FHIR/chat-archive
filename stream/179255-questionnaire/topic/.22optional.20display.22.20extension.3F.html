Topic: "optional display" extension?

Paul Lynch (Jul 09 2020 at 11:32):

I just noticed this optionalDisplay extension in SDC. It allows a Questionnaire item to say whether the renderer can choose to omit that item. What is the use case for that? When would someone making a Questionnaire not care whether an item was shown or not shown? Also, on what basis does the renderer decide whether to show optional items?

Lloyd McKenzie (Jul 09 2020 at 13:57):

The usecase was from CAP, where they define high-level questionnaires that local organizations are allowed to refine - including stripping out certain questions they don't feel are sufficiently useful/relevant. The extension is intended to flag those they're allowed to yank on derivation. If the use isn't clear from the description, feel free to submit a change request for us to boost the language

Yunwei Wang (Jul 09 2020 at 14:05):

One additional use case I can relate to my works is that when I have a really long questionnaire, I would like to "hide" those already answered items from display but still keep those answers in QR. @Paul Lynch We have talked about this before. Currently I use what you suggested by analyzing HTML tags. But I can see adding the omit=true for those already answered and trigger LHC to not displaying those.

Paul Lynch (Jul 09 2020 at 14:53):

I don't see how this extension would help in that case, since it goes in the Questionnaire definition, which is independent of the QRs.
