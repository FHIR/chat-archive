Topic: "options" and short vs. long lists

Paul Lynch (Aug 28 2018 at 18:43):

Is there a way for a form renderer to know whether the list behind the Questionnaire "options" URL is short or long (without requesting a full expansion)? I guess I am thinking of something like a "count" operation, though some lists perhaps won't have a complete count.

The reason I am asking is that If the list is short, it might be convenient to fully expand the list and display the ValueSet as radio buttons, or as a drop-down list in which all items are visible. If it is long, then it is preferable to use the filter parameter to a show only items that match what the user has typed (and not show anything until the user starts typing).

If one can't get this information about a ValueSet, is there a way for the Questionnaire to indicate how the list should be shown (i.e., fully expanded or just showing what matches)?

Lloyd McKenzie (Aug 28 2018 at 18:53):

The item-control extension lets you indicate whether something should be checkboxes/radio-button or dropdown or auto-complete. I think that sort of sets the expectation. Nothing technically stops you from saying "checkbox" for a list of 100 items - but I've seen questionnaires that do exactly that . So the notion of "short" vs. "long" is contextual and dependent on the designer.

Paul Lynch (Aug 28 2018 at 19:17):

True. That should work, and is unexpectedly flexible. Is there anything to prevent a Questionnaire from setting a question's itemControl to "autocomplete" when instead of options, "option" is used (specifying the choices directly in the Questionnaire)?

Lloyd McKenzie (Aug 28 2018 at 19:20):

Nope. Technically there's nothing to stop you from using "autocomplete" when the item type isn't even a choice, though that would be nonsensical...
