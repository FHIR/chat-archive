Topic: Change to WGM Connectathon registration

Lloyd McKenzie (Jun 26 2017 at 18:37):

HL7 has changed the process for connectathon registration. Previously, when you registered for the connectathon, your registration terminated at 12:30 on Sunday - so you weren't covered for lunch or the AID presentations/discussions on Sunday afternoon. Starting at the Sept. 2017 WGM, if you register for the connectathon, your registration will be good until the end of day Sunday - so your lunch is paid for, as are the afternoon sessions. So make your travel plans accordingly...

René Spronk (Jun 27 2017 at 07:39):

If you're (planning to) register for the FHIR connectathon in San Diego: the "hands on" connectathon part will be up to Lunch on Sunday (effectively 1.5 days); followed by the AID session Sunday PM.

AID is effectively the 'HL7 software implementers user group', which regularly host a series of "best practices/implementation experiences" presentations on Sunday PM. Mostly we'll have around 8 to 10 speakers, and 50 attendees for this session. Quite often these presentations will be video recorded, e.g. see https://vimeo.com/channels/hl7aid/page:5

This session is both for, as well as by, software implementers - as such: if you have anything of interest to share with your fellow implementers, just let me (or Andy Stechishin, or James Agnew - the AID co-chairs) know and we'll make sure you'll have a spot on our agenda. The agenda is fairly empty right now, http://wiki.hl7.org/index.php?title=AID_201709_Meeting_in_San_Diego , but in our experience it'll fill up quickly the closer we get (in time) to the actual FHIR connectathon.
