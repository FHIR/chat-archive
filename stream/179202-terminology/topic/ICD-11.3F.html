Peter Jordan (Mar 23 2021 at 21:10):

@David Pyke Yes, I believe that a JIRA ticket should be opened to correct that statement to something like this:
"The new version of the ICD (ICD-11) was released on June 18, 2018, as a preliminary version. It was officially presented at the World Health Assembly in May 2019 and will be used as the official reporting system by member states beginning January 1, 2022."
Given that ICD-11 appears to be very different from its predecessors, it might make sense to create a separate page in the FHIR Specification for "Using ICD-11 With FHIR". Rob Hausam (Mar 23 2021 at 21:56):

@Peter Jordan We definitely need to at the minimum update the ICD-[X] page so what it does say about ICD-11 is current. But I agree that having a separate page for ICD-11 is almost certainly warranted. The other considerations with all of that is that these updates/additions would only be in the R5 draft and the CI builds for some time - and instead what we really want to do is move all of this for external terminologies out of the FHIR spec so it can be accessed and managed separately, in the HTA pages and THO. But we also, I think, need to direct people to those sources or truth from the FHIR spec, as that's still the habit and also a very logical place for implementers to look first when they have questions about how to use terminology(ies) in FHIR.
