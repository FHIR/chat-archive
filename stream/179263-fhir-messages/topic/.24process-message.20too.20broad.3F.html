Topic: $process-message too broad?

Nick Hatt (Aug 30 2018 at 13:21):

I've been thinking about swagger specs recently, and it got me thinking $process-message is really broad.
There are only a handful of Operations defined at the global level: http://build.fhir.org/operationslist.html. Would it make sense to move process message to /MessageDefintion/[id]/$process-message?

Lloyd McKenzie (Aug 30 2018 at 13:47):

That would require the server to expose MessageDefinition in order to consume messages. A lot of message consumers won't expose any resources in REST at all...

Nick Hatt (Aug 30 2018 at 14:25):

In other words, MessageDefinition isn't technically required anywhere, so it shouldn't be part of the semantics. Is that right? Makes sense. I suppose an implementer could define custom message processing operations if it helped ergonomics.

Lloyd McKenzie (Aug 30 2018 at 14:30):

The system that exposes the MessageDefinition and the system that consumes the messages may well not be the same. Implementers can do all sorts of things, but leveraging the "standard" approach will generally be cheaper and easier for the community overall.
