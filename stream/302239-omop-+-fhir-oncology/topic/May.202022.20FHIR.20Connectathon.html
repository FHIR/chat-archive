Connectathon Track proposal has been submitted : https://confluence.hl7.org/display/FHIR/2022-05+FHIR-OMOP+Oncology
Please review and reach out with questions or feedback on how we can make this better.
Note that the use case is oncology-related but our work applies to any organization that focuses on translations from FHIR to OMOP. This is the only HL7/OHDSI partnership use case focused on translation from FHIR to OMOP - the others are converting from OMOP to FHIR. Therefore, we welcome any one that is interested, regardless of clinical domain, to join us in this exploration and effort.
Feel free to reach out to me if you have any questions or are interested in participating!

May Terry (Apr 12 2022 at 18:03):

The mCODE FHIR server used for the Connectathon now has 66 mCODE STU2 Conformant CancerPatient resources and 64 mCODE STU2 Conformant PrimaryCancerCondition resources. You can query them with the following FHIR REST API calls:

  • mCODE CancerPatient examples: https://api.logicahealth.org/mcodestu2/open/Patient?_profile=http://hl7.org/fhir/us/mcode/StructureDefinition/mcode-cancer-patient
  • mCODE PrimaryCancerCondition examples: https://api.logicahealth.org/mcodestu2/open/Condition?_profile=http://hl7.org/fhir/us/mcode/StructureDefinition/mcode-primary-cancer-condition

AJCC Cancer Stage Groups are represented inside PrimaryCancerCondition:Condition.stage.summary and will probably be the preferred representation for the purposes of this Connectathon.

I'll be working on converting Synthea -> mCODE STU2 FHIR examples for TumorMarkerTest and CancerRelatedMedicationAdministration next. In the meantime, feel free to start using what's in there now for your ETL logic or for just exploring/learning about mCODE FHIR resources.

Please post any questions here on this Zulip thread and I'll be happy to help you out.
