Lloyd McKenzie (Feb 17 2022 at 20:11):

No - I've needed to do it and my solution has been adding a comment in the differential. It does two things:
a) makes the element appear in the differential - which means the reader will pay some degree of attention to it - even if only to ask "why is this in the differential?"
b) adds the note saying "you 'SHOULD' support this - and ideally why"

If we ever get to the point of having multiple mustSupport flavors, then I guess this could be some sort of MS 'flavor'.

Richard Townley-O'Neill (Feb 18 2022 at 00:46):

You could mark the 'should support' elements with mustSupport = true and in your statement describing must support say "for the following elements 'must support' means 'should support'".

Must support does not have to mean the same thing for every element flagged as must support.

Josh Mandel (Feb 18 2022 at 15:10):

I know this is formally true. But what I want is at-a-glance documentation where developers can understand this SHALL/SHOULD/MAY type of logic without having to flip back and forth between different pages to cross reference the meaning of a flag.
