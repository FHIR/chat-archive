Fixing it for XHTML content is more straight-forward than for markdown (because it's easier to parse XHTML to see if you've got headings)

Lloyd McKenzie (May 20 2020 at 14:59):

As a general rule, pages that don't have enough content to have sub-headings are suspect. IGs should generally have a small number of pages with lots of content rather than a large number of pages with minimal content - because the latter design creates much more work for the reader.

Jean Duteau (May 20 2020 at 15:08):

That seems a strange thing to assert. Shouldn't it depend on the IG authors and readers as to how exactly an IG is laid out?

Lloyd McKenzie (May 20 2020 at 15:22):

I'm speaking from a 'best practice' perspective. Authors can create hundreds of small pages if they like, but the feedback I've seen from readers when faced with such IGs is that they don't generally like them. For example, it's much better to have a single use-cases page that displays several printed pages worth of use-case text than it is to have a master 'use-cases' page with hyperlinks to separate pages for each use-case, each of which is a couple of paragraphs. In the latter approach, the reader is constantly navigating back and forward just to be able to read through the use-cases. There's no reason to force that.
