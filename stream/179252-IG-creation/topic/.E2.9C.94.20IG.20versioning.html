Topic: ✔ IG versioning

Max Masnick (May 06 2021 at 13:17):

We are getting ready to create a "stable" release of our SMART Health Cards: Vaccination & Testing IG. This is still outside the HL7 process as our PSS is still making its way through the various approvals.

In our case, "stable" is just a signal to our implementers that we don't expect any breaking changes in the near-term -- it doesn't have any meaning from an HL7 perspective.

My question: What should the version number for this "stable" release be?

Right now, we have v0.4.0-rc for our current "release candidate." The natural thing to do from a semantic versioning perspective is for our "stable" release to be v1.0. But will this have negative implications down the road when we publish through HL7?

We want to avoid having to repeat or go backwards with the version numbers once this moves into HL7.

David Pyke (May 06 2021 at 13:17):

STU1 is typically 0.1.0

Grahame Grieve (May 06 2021 at 13:20):

STU1 is typically v1.0. And I think that there's very good reason for that. So your stable version should not be 1.0. typically, you won't be able to stop change when it goes through HL7. so you should not use something like 1.0. I wuld think 0.5 would do the job

Max Masnick (Jul 22 2021 at 13:31):

We are getting ready to turn over our IG content to our sponsoring WG in preparation for our STU 1 ballot.

At what point should we set the version number to v1.0 in the IG?

Right now our CI build says 0.6.0 - STU 1 (Proposed Ballot Version). I haven't bumped it up to v1.0 yet because we have implementers actively looking at the IG, and their assumption will be once it says v1.0 the content won't change until the version number changes again.

Max Masnick (Jul 22 2021 at 13:42):

We could do something like v1.0-release-candidate - STU 1 (Proposed Ballot Version) if that's acceptable from an HL7 standpoint

Lloyd McKenzie (Jul 22 2021 at 15:09):

1.0-ballot1 is what we're moving toward, but I don't know if the tooling supports that (both from a publishing perspective, but also a 'package management' perspective. Until @Grahame Grieve says otherwise, ballot releases should be a 0.x.0 release. The change to 1.0.0 comes during the QA period prior to final publication
