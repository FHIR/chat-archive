Lloyd McKenzie (Jan 03 2020 at 16:32):

Policy is: FCC is the preferred tool, but not (yet) mandated. And so long as we have Linux and other users who have challenges with it, FCC is unlikely to be mandated. The gate crasher is a known issue on many of our calls. Gotomeeting has an ability to 'lock' the meeting which keeps people from joining - so you can punt an offending user and not let them back in. We typically don't bother doing that unless the offending person is actually disrupting the call - which has happened occasionally, either by repeatedly playing hold music or by actually broadcasting porn audio. If they're just lurking, the best strategy seems to be to ignore them and not record them as present in the minutes. (They sometimes mascarade as well-known HL7 personalities, so It's always best to make sure that a name present on the web meeting is in fact a real person.)
