Dave deBronkart (Jul 23 2019 at 13:14):

A note about scope of my inquiry: when I first asked about this, I was thinking solely about end-user / consumer apps, which was also my perspective when thinking about patients in THIS community (chat.fhir.org) and more general discussions of patient involvement in FHIR.

A conceptual breakthrough happened when I realized that for DevDays (and probably for this chat community) patient innovators are a whole different beast. They, by definition, can be assumed to have interest in technical details, higher capacity for technical wording, etc. Rollout to the world of potential patient innovators is a whole different thing from rollout to consumers, and I propose we make that explicit and clear in our discussions within HL7, particularly the proposed patient innovator workgroup.

Abbie Watson (Aug 28 2019 at 05:06):

Catching up on old threads... @Virginia Lorenzi - sort of. Apple HealthRecord apps are storing records fetched via SMART on FHIR as JSON objects on the iPhone. HealthKit apps can access those records and get FHIR data types returned to them, but it involves a proprietary API. I would describe Apple apps as using FHIR data types and resources, but not abiding by the other aspects of the FHIR spec; certainly not the HTTP portion.
