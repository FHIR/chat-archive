Avnesh Ratnanesan (Jul 14 2021 at 07:54):

Hi @Dave deBronkart , thanks for your reply. You may not recall, we did meet once a few years when you presented a talk at HISA in Sydney and I interviewed you briefly - I think I've got the video somewhere!

With regards to your great questions, I've added more information here and website references. I was careful not to appear self-promoting, as that is not the intent of this discussion, and we are open to providing free licenses/open-sourcing the IP as part of this forum, and work out how to do it.

  1. The framework for patient experience is known as the 6e Methodology. It started off as a concept, which was well received then validated through education and training programs to healthcare managers in Australian hospitals since 2017. There's some high-level info on this methodology here https://energesse.com/training/

  2. Then since 2018/19, we began implementing the framework into an online technology platform, which can do a diagnostic of a hospitals capabilities in improving the patient experience and provide decision-support recommendations. 15 hospitals underwent trials using the product and we have some case studies recorded. We have a White Paper on this here (first button on the page for a free download) if anyone is interested. https://energesse.com/white-papers/

The data hierarchy for the diagnostic uses the 6e's as the first tier of domains, and breaks this analysis into 21 sub-domains, based on evidence-based practices. More info on that here https://energesse.com/patient-experience-maturity-evaluation-pxme/

  1. The definition of the patient experience that we typically refer to is the one from The Beryl Institute, i.e. " we define the patient experience as the sum of all interactions shaped by an organization's culture that influence patient perceptions across the continuum of care ".

I'd be happy to share more info on detailed case studies, etc in an off-line chat if anyone is interested in discussing this further.

Hope this is helpful,

Avi
