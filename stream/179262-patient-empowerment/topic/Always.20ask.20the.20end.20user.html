Topic: Always ask the end user

Dave deBronkart (Sep 20 2019 at 17:20):

Floating a principle here ...

In our informal talks this week we considered how we can provide value to others doing their work. It may be useful to compile some "principles of patient empowerment" or "Patient POV Principles" on Confluence.

In any case, here's one: Always ask the end user. (It's not limited to patients of course.) The rationale is that any developer may not be aware of how things show up in the end user's world.

My own example this morning is that when I went to refill a prescription, the name of my actual pharmacy did not show up, because it's called something else in the database! The lesson is that merely piping various "datums" onto a screen may not get the job done, in the end user's POV.

Here's the message I just posted in Social until it finds a better home.

Should we have a Principles document? (I tried to get on Confluence last night during my travels, on phone apps, and was not able to connect, so I haven't deposited a single bit of anything yet.)
