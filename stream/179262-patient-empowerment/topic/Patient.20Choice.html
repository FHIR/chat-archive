John Moehrke (Apr 21 2020 at 12:59):

we do have a huge number of vocabularies... These are engaged in Consent resource use of Permit vs Deny rules.

  • Categories of sensitive health information http://build.fhir.org/v3/ActCode/cs.html#v3-ActCode-_ActInformationSensitivityPolicy
  • categories of data that have a personal sensitivity http://build.fhir.org/v3/ActCode/cs.html#v3-ActCode-_EntitySensitivityPolicyType
  • categories of data relationships that have a sensitivity http://build.fhir.org/v3/ActCode/cs.html#v3-ActCode-_RoleInformationSensitivityPolicy
  • risk classification of sensitive health information http://build.fhir.org/v3/ConfidentialityClassification/vs.html
  • reason categories for intended data use http://build.fhir.org/v3/PurposeOfUse/vs.html
    These are the topic of some efforts to make their use more clear. This clarity is not necessarily specific to Consent, but certainly the clarity is helpful in consent. -- see the currently open ballot for public comment on FHIR DS4P
    In the FHIR core this all is covered under security labels - http://build.fhir.org/security-labels.html
    Which is linked to from things like Consent.provision[*].securityLabel

John Moehrke (Apr 21 2020 at 13:09):

That said... Consent has other vocabulary based vectors

  • class -- typically based on LOINC or SNOMED ontology
  • code -- specific codes from medical terminology
  • data period -- some date/time start-stop that the patient wants to include (Permit) or exclude (Deny)
  • Individual -- indicator of organization, careteam, group, practitioner, related person --- that the patient wants to include (Permit) or exclude (Deny)

John Moehrke (Apr 21 2020 at 13:11):

All on a infinitely recursive rules language.
E.g. Permit Treatment, but not Dr Bob, unless Dr Bob is the last person on earth he can see the medication list, but not my alcohol abuse related meds.
