Lloyd McKenzie (Nov 22 2017 at 18:16):

"Mr." is part of the string that you'd use when printing out someone's name - just like "Dr." or "Professor" or "Miss" or any of a number of other things. If it's part of the name string, it must be a name part, and it certainly fits the criteria for being a "prefix". There can be different types of prefixes and suffixes that indicate a variety of things - gender, marital status, professional status, education, etc. If you just have "prefix", there's no way to tell what the meaning is. There are extensions that can let you differentiate though. Look at http://build.fhir.org/extension-iso21090-en-qualifier.html

René Spronk (Nov 23 2017 at 06:31):

We've had this discussion in a v2 context, as well as in v3. Which just goes to show that different contexts/countries deal with this in different ways. In quite a few European countries Mr/Ms is auto-generated based on the value of Patient.gender, and not part of the name itself. In Sweden they check whether the national person number is odd or even, which is an indication of gender and thus an indication of whether Mr or Ms should be used. Quite often for minors the use of Mr/Ms is omitted, and the full given name is used. All of this is supported by the FHIR datatype, how one uses it is up to the context.

René Spronk (Nov 23 2017 at 06:33):

Note that Dr. would be part of the name in most European countries I know of (the discussion above is solely about Mr/Ms)
