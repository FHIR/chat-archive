Stream: implementers

Topic: "De-activating" resources

Shlomy Reinstein (Feb 13 2017 at 13:36):

In some use cases, I may need to mark some FHIR resources as "inactive", so that applications will no longer use them. I don't want to delete the resources, since they might have been in use at some point, so, for auditing purposes, I want to keep the resources. For this purpose, I need a way to mark the resources "inactive".

Shlomy Reinstein (Feb 13 2017 at 13:36):

Is there a standard way to mark resources as inactive?

Shlomy Reinstein (Feb 13 2017 at 13:37):

I need this mainly for Condition resources, but also for some other resources.

Lloyd McKenzie (Feb 13 2017 at 16:30):

What does "inactive" mean from a clinical perspective for your Condition resource?

Shlomy Reinstein (Feb 19 2017 at 06:33):

Normally it would mean that the data was entered in error, and now has to be corrected.

Lloyd McKenzie (Feb 20 2017 at 06:06):

Most resouces have an "entered-in-error" status. All resources must declare how the "entered in error" situation is handled - you can see information about that here: http://build.fhir.org/lifecycle.html#error

Shlomy Reinstein (Mar 21 2017 at 09:44):

Is there a more general solution to deactivating resources without specifying the reason? The reason could be either "entered-in-error" as above, or it could be that the resources are no longer used, for example, if we sort of "mirror" a non-FHIR EMR in a FHIR server, and something got removed from the EMR, we want to deactivate the resource. We don't know the reason that the data was removed from EMR.

John Moehrke (Mar 21 2017 at 11:59):

You can always explain this in the Provenance resource you attach to the update/delete you make.

Shlomy Reinstein (Mar 21 2017 at 13:39):

I don't understand the answer. How do I mark the resources as "deactivated", so I can then exclude them from queries?

John Moehrke (Mar 21 2017 at 14:03):

The universal way is to Delete them... when servers support versioning, this is not necessarly a bad thing. However some objects are best kept around, and marked (status element). There is ongoing work in FHIR on workflow, to create a more consistent way to 'lifecyle' these kinds of objects that are best kept around. There is some consistency in STU3, but this is still a work-in-progress. Your comments are very helpful toward his work-in-progress.

Shlomy Reinstein (Mar 28 2017 at 08:00):

If the server supports versioning, does it mean that deleting a resource will just cause it to be marked 'deleted' on the server, but old versions can still be retrieved?

Lloyd McKenzie (Mar 28 2017 at 13:42):

Yes
