Lloyd McKenzie (Feb 12 2020 at 20:30):

In some cases, there won't ever be bindings on an international - or even a national - basis. E.g. billing codes. There's just no chance of getting consensus or being able to point at a publicly available example set. The standardization of terminology varies by area and by country. What HL7 can lock down it does, but it can't lock down very much. Implementation guides like US Core can (and do) lock down more, but even they can't lock down everything. Something like valueCodeableConcept can really only be meaningfully constrained in the context of a specific Observation.code. You won't find a single value set that covers the components of an APGAR, the different types of genetic variants and the possible values for the questions in a psychiatric assessment in a single value set.
