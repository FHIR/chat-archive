I have asked a similar question on another topic but marked it as a resolved topic because the focus of that question was based on the Patient resource. So I will ask a similar question but this time with the focus on the _element parameter.

Suppose that we are going to retrieve all patients who have at least one QuestionnaireResponse.item.answer = 'A' AND the corresponding question (i.e., Questionnaire.item.text) = 'Q'".

Is that wise/doable to use the _element parameter like the following to meet the above-mentioned request:

[Base]/QuestionnaireResponse?item.answer=A&questionnaire.item.text=Q&_elements=patient

Lee Surprenant (Jan 26 2022 at 13:50):

QuestionnaireResponse has no top-level element named "patient" and so this query is not correct. You could replace "patient" with "subject" and then I think it would work. In general, _elements is just a way to get SUBSETTED resources back...I'd think of it as an optimization.

David Pyke (Jan 26 2022 at 14:59):

item is not normally a searchable parameter, although it's possible that a system may support it. So, searching by individual response to a question is unlikely to work. Likley, you would have to search for all patients that have answered the linked questionnaire and then query all those questionnaireResponses and filter down client-side to those that answered the way you wanted
