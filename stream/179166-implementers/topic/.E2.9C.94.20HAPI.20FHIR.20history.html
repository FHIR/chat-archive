Florian Boemmel (Aug 31 2021 at 07:51):

Hi guys,
I'm pretty new to fhir and the hapi framework (version 5.3.0). What i want to do is to read a fhir resource with a IGenericClient.

    return getRestfulClient()
        .read()
        .resource(resourceClass)
        .withId(id)
        .execute();

getRestfulCilent does some extra work and provides a IGenericClient. resourceClass is for example Practitioner.

Basically this works pretty good and i get the resource. In the meta we need to set the versionId field to e.g. 1. What i noticed, the HAPI framework adds then automatically "_history/1" to the id field. In general the number in versionId will be added at the end of the history tag.

Is there a flag or other call to avoid this behavior? I searched already for this but found nothing...
Thanks in advance!

Lloyd McKenzie (Aug 31 2021 at 13:48):

You can't control the version number assigned to the resource - that's driven by the server. The version can't be 'set'. And it won't necessarily even be a sequential number.
