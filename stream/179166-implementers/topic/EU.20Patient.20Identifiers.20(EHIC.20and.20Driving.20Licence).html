René Spronk (Jun 16 2017 at 08:37):

A Driving License is issued by an individual country, and as such one would have (at least) 1 URL per country. IHIC leverages the person id number as used by the member country (ditto, at least 1 URL assigned by the member state).
IHIC has the "identification number of the card" - now that would be a EU wide id scheme. Perhaps @Alexander Henket knows the URL for it - I haven't searched for it, but I also don't happen to have seen it up to now.

Alexander Henket (Jun 16 2017 at 08:44):

Questions exists more than there are answers here. We never solved it for OIDs in V3 and may never solve it for system URIs in FHIR.

EHIC Policy ID, EHIC Insurance Company ID, Driver's License and even Passport Number have so far found no place in any cross border use case for us so we lacked incentive to solve it.

For Passport Number there at least is an OID scheme "2.16.840.1.113883.4.330" + ISO country number from 3166-1. It might be that the solution for the other concepts is along the same lines, but we never got to that

Alexander Henket (Jun 16 2017 at 08:46):

Please note that in general, inventing new system URIs where OIDs exist and are implemented is a big nuisance and a potential for interoperability problems. -- Anyone who knows me, knew I needed to say this… after > 10 years of educating our implementers on OIDs we are being butchered over the introduction of system URIs for the exact same thing that we have been using OIDs for all these years.
