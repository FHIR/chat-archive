Lloyd McKenzie (Sep 22 2020 at 21:46):

Typically negative assertions show up as formal Observations only if they're specifically 'pertinent' to care. So something like "no family history of heart disease" would be perfectly reasonable to have as an Observation. Similarly negative findings of susceptibility tests would be captured as Observations. However, if you had a questionnaire that asked people to check off all conditions that they had a family history of, you wouldn't typically extract an Observation of "no family history of X" for everything they didn't click on. (You could, but as mentioned, it would be noisy for most clinical use.) The short answer is that if you need to represent a negative finding, you use the same resource. So "no allergy to X" would be an AllergyIntolerance with a status of refuted. "No family history of X" would be an Observation. "Cancer free" would be a Condition with a status of refuted, remission or resolved (depending on the clinical context), etc.
