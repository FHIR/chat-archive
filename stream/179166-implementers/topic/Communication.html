Ewout Kramer (Mar 14 2016 at 19:33):

I remember when we designed this part of Patient - we agreed that language proficiency was outside "the 80%", i.e. only interesting to a small part of the implementer community. So, you need extensions to convey proficiency for now.

Lloyd McKenzie (Mar 14 2016 at 22:54):

You could submit a change request for the WG to define a standard extension.

Brian Postlethwaite (Mar 15 2016 at 01:06):

Brian Postlethwaite (Mar 18 2016 at 00:32):

There is a standard extension for using in this use case where the patient is identified as not being able to speak the native language assumed by the system.
http://hl7.org/fhir/extension-patient-interpreterrequired.html
And then the languages lists in the communication provide the detail of which languages are available, and hence which interpreter to get.
