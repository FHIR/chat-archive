Topic: ✔ RequestGroup Action Resource: Goal vs Task Referencing ...

Kyle Thompson (Nov 10 2021 at 17:56):

Does it make sense to set action.resource in a RequestGroup to a Goal or would it make sense to have action.reference point to a Task whose focus in a Goal?

Bryn Rhodes (Nov 10 2021 at 21:28):

The action.resource should be pointing to a Request or Event resource that represents the activity being performed. In R5, we've added goal elements to the RequestGroup and RequestGroup.action, so you could use a pre-adoption extension to reference the goal directly in an R4 RequestGroup.

Kyle Thompson (Nov 10 2021 at 22:22):

OK, that makes sense to store the Goal on the Action in the RequestGroup.

From what I understand, Task's can be used to both represent a Request and track the fulfillment of that request. Is that correct? If it is correct, could I reference a Task in a RequestGroup action where the Task references the Goal as its focus?

Bryn Rhodes (Nov 10 2021 at 23:49):

Goal still doesn't feel like the right target for Task.focus to me. The definition for focus is "The request being actioned or the resource being manipulated by this task.", since the Goal isn't what's being acted on or manipulated, that doesn't seem right. Seems more like a "reasonReference", since the Goal is why the Task is being created, or at least what is trying to be accomplished.

Bryn Rhodes (Nov 10 2021 at 23:49):

Focus would be something more like a ServiceRequest or a Procedure, or something like that.

Kyle Thompson (Nov 11 2021 at 13:28):

That makes perfect sense. Thanks so much for clarifying!

Lloyd McKenzie (Nov 15 2021 at 05:44):

Task.focus could be a Goal if the objective of the Task is getting someone to manipulate the Goal (e.g. because the requester doesn't have authority to do so)

Lloyd McKenzie (Nov 18 2021 at 00:29):

Right.
