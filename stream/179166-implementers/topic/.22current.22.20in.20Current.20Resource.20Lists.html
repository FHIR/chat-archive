Topic: "current" in Current Resource Lists

Amy Wilson (Nov 14 2016 at 03:20):

Hello there, looking for some clarification on how "current" in https://www.hl7.org/fhir/lifecycle.html#current is being interpreted
Is a "current problem" one which is affecting the patient at the moment? With "past diagnoses" being ones the patient no longer has (some of which will be "relevant to the current care of the patient" and some not)

Is there room for a broader definition of "current"? We are aggregating problems across different sources and some we will mark as duplicates because they are in more than one source. Could we say that these are "not current" (even though they represent a problem that the patient has at the present time)?

Grahame Grieve (Nov 14 2016 at 03:26):

There is no generally agreed definition of 'current'. It means whatever the local system means (or it's users) when things are put on the current list
