Topic: "Display" string for Identifier URIs

Ken Sinn (Oct 18 2016 at 17:56):

Hi everyone,

Identifiers typically have an URI to represent the assigning system [identifier.system], and the actual ID itself [identifier.value].
Ideally the URI would resolve, or would be canonically obvious. Even in such cases, URIs can be lengthy (in characters).

Is there any way to encode a "display" value associated with a URI? I know that URI are defined as a Primitive Type, so maybe this question targets the Identifier Complex Type. Is there a way to specify "what" the Identifier.system value represents, in display/textual form? Identifier.type seems intended to designate "Medical Record Number" or "Health Card Number", but there's no way to identify that the identifier is "MRN from St.Johns Hospital, Toronto" or "Ontario Health Card Number", aside from pre-defining the expected URIs.

Would welcome thoughts/ideas ,thank you!

Grahame Grieve (Oct 18 2016 at 19:42):

typically, you an use Identiifer.type.text or Identiifer.assigner.display for this

Lloyd McKenzie (Oct 19 2016 at 01:48):

Identifier.type.text describes specifics of the identifier. Identifier.assigner.display can only convey information about the assigning organization, not the identifier itself.
