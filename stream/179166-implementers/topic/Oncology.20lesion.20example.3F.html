David McKillop (Jun 14 2016 at 23:08):

@Tilo Christ the Royal College of Pathologists of Australasia (RCPA) have developed a number of structured reporting of cancer protocols which can be found at:
https://www.rcpa.edu.au/Library/Practising-Pathology/Structured-Pathology-Reporting-of-Cancer/Cancer-Protocols
with a specific one on lung cancer at:
https://www.rcpa.edu.au/getattachment/91579f39-e6b0-4c55-81f6-b845674bffe9/Protocol-lung-cancer.aspx
The RCPA have a current project using the colorectal cancer protocol and the prostate cancer protocol which are being modelling using FHIR resources.
Colorectal: https://hl7-fhir.github.io/nehta/nehta-colorectal.html
Prostate: https://hl7-fhir.github.io/nehta/nehta-prostate-radical.html
This is a pilot project where a selected public and private laboratory will be transmitting FHIR artefacts to a selected cancer registry. A subsequent project may look at expanding the development of further cancer protocols.
