Johannes Becker (Jan 27 2022 at 16:47):

When a Timing specifies a repetition and has no explicit start datetime specified, what's the expected start datetime? 12am as with crontab?

Lloyd McKenzie (Jan 27 2022 at 21:37):

There is no specific start time. If someone writes a prescription and says "take this 3 times a day" and doesn't specify a start date, the general expectation is "as soon as you can". But that might mean hours or days in practice. (In the community scenario, patient sticks the script into their pocket, brings it to the grocery drug store the next week, pharmacy is out, so they come back the next week to get their supply, don't start taking it until the next day... In an in-patient environment, it's typically "next scheduled administration timeslot that corresponds to the schedule" - unless instructions or protocol dictate otherwise.
