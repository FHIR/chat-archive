Lloyd McKenzie (Apr 30 2018 at 13:27):

Develop FHIR resources per category: Not sure what you're looking for here - take a look at the resource list and see if you can find what you're looking for. If you have problems deciding, ask specific questions here.
Modes of connectivity(Mutual TLS); Security aspects on each API calls: FHIR supports and encourages TLS but doesn't mandate it - or any other particular security approach. Security should be based on your particular implementation's risk. Take a look at the security page to get started
Ideal performance of all API resources: FHIR is totally silent here. Some implementations may need milliseconds or less. Others will be fine with a second or two. Others will use asynchronous processing. You'll have to find out what's necessary/appropriate for your communication partners
Is there any pre-requisites which I must be adhere on it before exposing my FHIR APIs for all healthcare vendors?: Read the section on conformance
Is there any requirements of certification for this implementation?: We don't yet offer certification of systems. When/if we do, it'll probably be certification against specific implementation guides rather than against the base specification.

There is no "single" FHIR workflow for that process because there's no single clinical workflow for that process. It varies by country, by system and sometimes by clinican or type of image. Where can I find updated data about the degree of adoption of V2, V3 and FHIR?

Lloyd McKenzie (Jul 18 2018 at 17:37):

V2, V3 and FHIR are completely different standards that are managed in very different ways. There's no equivalent to "resource" in the v3 world. Instead it has interactions and RMIMs. And those are maintained in separate chapters. You can find all of the ones defined by HL7 international in the v3 Normative Edition, but much of the actual v3 messaging implementation has happened against country-specific interactions and RMIMs and there's no central index of those. v2 has segments and messages and those can be found in the HL7 v2 specification.

Unfortunately it's very difficult for HL7 to track adoption of its standards. There's been the odd study done on v2 adoption in various geographies, but I'm not aware of anything recent. And I'm not familiar with any reports that gather that information for v3 or FHIR (and FHIR is changing so rapidly that any information gathered would quickly be out of date).
