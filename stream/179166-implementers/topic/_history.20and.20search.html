Topic: _history and search

René Spronk (Oct 26 2018 at 15:24):

During a FHIR training course the trainees were asked to get hold of the history of an Observation, and one of them came up with GET vonk.fire.ly/Observation?patient.identifier=1020304050/_history - this returns a bundle with 0 entries (whereas GET vonk.fire.ly/Observation?patient.identifier=1020304050 has multiple matches) .
Their approach mixes search with _history. The spec is silent on this. What would the expected behavior have to be ?

Lloyd McKenzie (Oct 26 2018 at 15:27):

That expression isn't defined within the FHIR spec. As such, it's not strictly illegal - a server could offer up a response there. But it's also legitimate to raise an error.

Brian Postlethwaite (Oct 27 2018 at 01:22):

I would expect that sine the / is to the right of the ?, then it would be interpreted as a part of the query, not the path.
So search for an observation where the patient identifier was 1020304050/_history which I would suggest wasn't a valid id.

Brian Postlethwaite (Oct 27 2018 at 01:22):

so many places there that a server might complain about that query.

Christiaan Knaap (Dec 17 2018 at 11:26):

Identifier.value has no restrictions on its format, so Vonk simply searches for identifier 1020304050/_history, which happens not to be there. You are equally allowed to POST a Patient with that as its identifier.

Brian Postlethwaite (Dec 18 2018 at 11:38):

Good point Christiaan
