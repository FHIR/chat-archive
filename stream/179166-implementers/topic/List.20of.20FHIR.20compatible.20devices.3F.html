Mikael Rinnetmäki (Jan 04 2020 at 08:17):

I've worked in this space quite a bit, and haven't encountered a single device that would natively support FHIR.
However, many of this kind of devices nowadays have companion apps, and I'm hearing some of the apps are getting FHIR support some time in the future. Same for cloud services offered by device manufacturers (which expose the data from the device uploaded by the companion app).

Today, the most common scenario is that you have third party apps and services that communicate with the device directly (through USB, Zigbee, or Bluetooth, for instance) or with a cloud service of the device vendor, and then convert that data to FHIR. I've personally been involved in a few projects of this kind. I also know of other projects I haven't been involved in. Also, the #finnish PHR platform is all about having data from these personal monitoring devices available for citizens and healthcare practitioners through apps, in FHIR format.
