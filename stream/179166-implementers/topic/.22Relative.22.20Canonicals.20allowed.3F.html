Jean Duteau (Aug 14 2019 at 02:56):

I had another good question in a tutorial today - can the meta.profile be a relative URI? I couldn't see why not but it just didn't seem right to me. There is a lot of talk about canonical in the spec, but it doesn't actually say whether we expect a data element of the canonical datatype to be an absolute URI or whether it is allowed to be relative.

Michel Rutten (Aug 14 2019 at 08:49):

Hi @Jean Duteau, canonical urls must be globally unique and (therefore) absolute:
http://hl7.org/fhir/structuredefinition-definitions.html#StructureDefinition.url
In theory, systems could accept relative urls within a limited scope (e.g. dev environment), as long as they are unique within that scope. However for published conformance resources, canonical urls must be absolute. Use a domain that is under your control, e.g. your company url. For examples resources, it is common practice to use "example.org".
