Ted Klein (Oct 27 2021 at 15:40):

  1. As per HTA approved and TSC endorsed policy, NO code system published by an organization outside of HL7 will have codes published in THO UNLESS an explicit agreement to do so, and a process for maintaining current accurate content in the face of external updates is defined and documented;

Ted Klein (Oct 27 2021 at 15:45):

  1. All of those external code system metadata records you see that are in the category of "Other" (meaning not curated by HTA) are subject to imminent removal. This is as per an email that went out to all cochairs (I think probably not to the IC reps which was probably an oversight, please contact @Reuben Daniels about it). These were all imported and their fields assigned pro forma by the import algorithm from material published in the V3 coremif release December 2019. In almost 2 years no one has cleaned them up, so HTA sent a request for any that people wish to remain in THO be identified and cleaned up, all others will be removed next month. I am pretty sure all of those ICPC variants are among that pile. Note also that the way the variants are represented in FHIR differs from V3, which did not support the concept of code system supplements. Hence what you are seeing is the pro forma representation of the v3 model there. If you feel these should be part of the THO publication, please notify HTA to this effect, and please take on the task of fixing them.

Ted Klein (Oct 27 2021 at 15:46):

  1. See 1 and 2 above. Any code system that is not authored and maintained by HL7 through the UTG process must come through HTA, and content can only be put in there as per agreement, again through HTA. @Carol Macumber and @Reuben Daniels are the ones to contact about this.
