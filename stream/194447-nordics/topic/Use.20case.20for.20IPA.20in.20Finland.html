Topic: Use case for IPA in Finland

Mikael Rinnetmäki (May 22 2021 at 09:31):

Finland is currently passing legislation that includes the mandate for citizens to get access to their health data from the national Kanta registry, thgough apps. We've had a patient portal for viewing the data for many years, this change is about API access to that data.

Mikael Rinnetmäki (May 22 2021 at 09:32):

Details are at https://www.eduskunta.fi/FI/vaski/HallituksenEsitys/Sivut/HE_212+2020.aspx and https://www.eduskunta.fi/FI/vaski/HallituksenEsitys/Documents/HE_212+2020.pdf. Both in Finnish, but Google Translate might be your friend.

Mikael Rinnetmäki (May 22 2021 at 09:32):

Especially:

  • 20 § Potilastietojen luovuttaminen valtakunnallisten tietojärjestelmäpalvelujen avulla Potilastiedot voidaan luovuttaa asiakkaalle hyvinvointisovelluksen tai kansalaisen käyttöliittymän kautta. Saadakseen tiedot hyvinvointisovellukseen potilaan tulee ottaa hyvinvointisovellus käyttöön ja hyväksyä tietojen luovutus. (page 167 in the PDF file, recitals pages 103-105) 
  • 52 § Siirtymäsäännökset  Lain 20 §:n 4 momenttia sovelletaan hyvinvointisovellusten osalta viimeistään 1 päivänä joulukuuta 2022. (page 180 in the PDF file) 

In essence: citizens can download their patient data with an app. This shall be possible on December 1, 2022 at the latest.

Mikael Rinnetmäki (May 22 2021 at 09:32):

We’ve made some noise about the existence of the IPA initiative, and have gotten good feedback, and IPA is present on architectural scetches on how this could be implemented (by Kela, the national institution responsible for implementation).

Mikael Rinnetmäki (May 22 2021 at 09:33):

Surely, though, the legislation does not mention IPA, HL7, or even interoperability.

Mikael Rinnetmäki (May 22 2021 at 09:33):

Now, in my view one risk is that if IPA is not considered to be “ready”, we’ll use something else instead – with no real interoperability with other systems.

Mikael Rinnetmäki (May 22 2021 at 09:34):

Another risk is that we’ll take the IPA draft and fill in the missing gaps and adopt it to our needs freely and think it is IPA – with no real interoperability with other systems.

Mikael Rinnetmäki (May 22 2021 at 09:34):

Right now would be the time to state that there is this group of countries, ministeries, HL7 affiliates, companies, or individuals, either interested in or committed to working towards an interoperable IPA specification.

Mikael Rinnetmäki (May 22 2021 at 09:34):

We could then tie that activity to Kela’s implementation, and use that as the first reference implementation.

Mikael Rinnetmäki (May 22 2021 at 09:36):

Why does this matter to me?

  • As an app developer, I want to see a harmonized way of accessing health data, globally.
  • The US already has a pretty good baseline with US Argonaut. Extended to Canada and UK? At least Apple Health is. We should aim to make this a global approach.
  • Giving patients access to their data breaks down many of the barriers preventing data sharing today.
