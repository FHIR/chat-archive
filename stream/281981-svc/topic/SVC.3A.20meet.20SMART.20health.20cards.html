Peter Jordan (May 20 2021 at 02:14):

Judging from the recent Global Consortium for eHealth Interoperability (HL7, IHE & HIMSS) meeting, there appears to be some appetite for alignment between 3 of the major global initiatives - Digital Green Certificate (EU), Vaccine Credential Initiative (US vendors & 'tech titans') and Smart Vaccination Certificates (WHO). One notable absence was the IATA (Travel Pass Initiative).

The 3 groups who presented at the meeting all cited use of FHIR, to some degree, even to the level of using the IPS Immunization Profile, although there was an indication from the VCI Stream at the Connectathon today that the WHO is no longer doing that (not sure if that's related to the contentious ICD-11 v SNOMED CT terminology issue).

Carl Leitner (May 20 2021 at 14:06):

FYI: the only place we would be using custom resources is to condense the json representations of a QResp before encoding in a QR code (e.g. as a part of the serialization step). This can shave 2-3 off the version size of a QR code over a raw QResp. Plan is to have mappings from WHO to DGC, ICAO (or at least for the subset of data that is within the WHO coredata set - ICAO for example have required travel document ID which WHO won't have)
