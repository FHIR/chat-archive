Topic: "Active" Organization

Jay Lyle (Feb 24 2022 at 15:46):

When populating a coverage eligibility request, we use Organization. US Core Organization requires "active", but our Organization data may be stale.

Is it preferable to assert "active: true (as of the last time we knew, which may have been a long time ago)", or to DAR the element as unknown?

Josh Mandel (Feb 24 2022 at 16:49):

but our Organization data may be stale.

I think Jay's saying that in this state, an organization's "active" status is best described as "unknown" rather than "true" or "false".

Josh Mandel (Feb 24 2022 at 23:21):

But if the issue is "the Org says active=true but the data may be stale" rather than "we don't know the active status", then passing along a status and providing a timestamp so clients can evaluate freshness is OK too -- we approached a similar issue for SMART Scheduling Links using a lastSourceSync core extension, to explicitly convey the last synced time. See FHIR-31567 for details.

We will add a new extension on meta "lastSourceSync" as dateTime:

Definition: "Indicates when the data was last accessed from its original source"

Comment: "Relevant only on data that propagated from a system other than the current system."
