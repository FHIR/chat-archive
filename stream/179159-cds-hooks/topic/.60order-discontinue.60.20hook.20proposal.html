Topic: `order-discontinue` hook proposal

Isaac Vetter (Dec 28 2021 at 17:07):

Hey CDS Hooks folks!

Following a conversation last week in the CDS wg, I wrote up a PR to propose a new order-discontinue hook. The idea here is that order-sign doesn't make sense, and order-select's specific context data structures don't align well with a discontinue workflow.

Isaac Vetter (Dec 28 2021 at 17:07):

Some outstanding questions -- an order-discontinue hooks makes a ton of sense for med prescriptions, also for standing orders (e.g. weekly labs, recurring infusions, etc). But should this apply to a one-time event, like a procedure? What's the difference between cancel and discontinue?

Isaac Vetter (Dec 28 2021 at 17:07):

Additionally, how do systems treat parent and child orders? I think that the primary value is in firing this hook for parent orders.

Isaac Vetter (Dec 28 2021 at 17:07):

Community review requested!

John Silva (Dec 28 2021 at 18:25):

From table 0119 - Order Status codes ( https://www.hl7.org/fhir/v2/0119/index.html )

"The differentiation between discontinue and cancel is that discontinue effects the order/service and all future occurrences, cancel refers to just the present action."

Lloyd McKenzie (Jan 03 2022 at 05:05):

Would this hook fire when an order expires naturally? I.e. status changes to 'complete' or only when it terminates abnormally?

Isaac Vetter (Jan 03 2022 at 19:01):

Hey Lloyd, only for an unexpected, manual termination. That would make the most sense, right?

Re: discontinue & cancel. Could the request resource sent in toBeDiscontinued enable the service to differentiate between these two scenarios? (e.g. send child request for cancels, parent for discontinues?) Do y'all know how do systems handle this today?
