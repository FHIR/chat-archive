Topic: how mature are our hooks?

Isaac Vetter (Dec 12 2018 at 22:39):

Feedback from the CDS Hooks ballot earlier this year, resulted in this Hook Maturity Model which guides a hook author through the process of maturing a proposed hook and clearly labels the maturity level.

Isaac Vetter (Dec 12 2018 at 22:39):

We're now faced with the challenge of labeling our hooks that pre-existed before this model. We've got a gh issue and PR queued up to state that patient-view is the most mature (and is almost ready for a ballot), medication-prescribe is immature, but has seen testing, and order-reviewis mostly untested. Is this accurate?

Richard Ettema (Dec 13 2018 at 00:06):

If you consider the Da Vinci Project's Coverage Requirements Discovery (CRD) IG, you'll see that it defines use cases for both medication-prescribe and order-review, as well as new hooks for appointment-booking, encounter-start and encounter-discharge. The MITRE RI for the CRD IG currently supports medication-prescribe and order-review.

Alex Tatiyants (Dec 13 2018 at 01:28):

Is there a path for retiring hooks in this model? For example, there have been a lot of discussions about medication-prescribe being an unnecessary version of order-review. Speaking of order-review, I'm aware of at least 2 EMRs who've implemented this hook either in production or close to it. I would consider it at least documented.

Isaac Vetter (Dec 13 2018 at 05:56):

new hooks for appointment-booking, encounter-start and encounter-discharge

Thanks, @Richard Ettema . The CRD IG is very thoroughly written! This Hook Maturity Model is how we should evaluate maturity. We need to get these CRD-proposed written up on cds-hooks.org. It seems like there's a thorough plan for testing the MITRE RI's and other CRD implementations. As that testing progresses, we should use it to also advance stated maturity according to this schema.
