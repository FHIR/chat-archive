Discussion: override reasons.

Josh Mandel (Sep 15 2019 at 19:47):

There are examples of clinician actions that dismiss a card that aren't really "overriding" (e.g., "I'm asking my colleague to address this")

Is there a difference between existing suggestions capability (with a notice --> feedback service when selected) vs "override reasons" (which also would have a notice --> feedback service when selected) ?

Josh Mandel (Sep 15 2019 at 19:50):

Often a decision to "snooze" a card should come with a reason/explanation (e.g., patient is on chemotherapy right now).

Josh Mandel (Sep 15 2019 at 19:50):

There are lots of options, re: defining a catalog of responses. Should they be standardized?

Josh Mandel (Sep 15 2019 at 19:51):

Is it possible to capture the "override reason", for example in a FHIR resource that the EHR will keep?

Josh Mandel (Sep 15 2019 at 19:53):

e.g. if a patient declines colonoscopy because it's too expensive. It'd be nice to make this known to future services, too (?).

Josh Mandel (Sep 15 2019 at 19:56):

The CDS Service might be able to generate a list of common refusal reasons (e.g., patient preference; cost; contraindication; etc).

Josh Mandel (Sep 15 2019 at 19:58):

Alternatively, the EHR might understand suggestions (e.g., through tags/codings) well enough to remember reasons over time and suppress future cards.

Josh Mandel (Sep 15 2019 at 19:59):

Can we separate concerns?

  • Services focused on providing decision logic (e.g., the rules about when colonoscopy is appropriate)
  • Pedigree of events, like which suggestions were received, declined, etc

Josh Mandel (Sep 15 2019 at 20:03):

Difference between tracking of what the user clicked; vs ultimately whether the orders were actually placed.

Josh Mandel (Sep 15 2019 at 20:08):

Overall: do we think acknowledgements/overrides can represent useful information that provides a better clinical experience? Simplistic but useful?

Josh Mandel (Sep 15 2019 at 20:26):

Looking ahead to performance:

  • What are the things we can to improve ability of services to return quickly?
  • What are the things we can do to improve UX when services return slowly?

Josh Mandel (Sep 15 2019 at 20:27):

Often CDS performance may be gated by the time required to request resources from a FHIR server (i.e., gathering inputs)

Josh Mandel (Sep 15 2019 at 21:01):

For ordering: how to manage groups of orders (order sets? pre-filled checkboxes?)
