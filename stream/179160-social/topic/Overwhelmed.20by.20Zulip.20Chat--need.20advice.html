Rob Hausam (Jun 08 2018 at 16:52):

Hi, Lisa. There are a number of us who can offer suggestions and help. Mostly I think it just takes some time and gaining familiarity. We do use it a lot as a database of prior responses, one that we continue to build up on the fly. And, as David said, it also works pretty well as a real-time chat (although I do still also use Skype sometimes for that). I think you will probably be better satisfied with using the app in the long run, but both certainly work. For figuring out what's been discussed already there are probably several strategies, but I tend to use a combination of browsing through the topics in a stream and then diving in to ones that look relevant/interesting, and/or searching with likely keywords/phrases in streams or topics that might likely have what I'm looking for.

Lloyd McKenzie (Jun 08 2018 at 17:59):

If you click on the magnifying glass icon beside the search space, it provides you with a cheat-sheet for searching. Lots of people ask questions without searching, so even if you do a quick cursory search, you're better behaved than most. I typically use search when I know something has been previously discussed. I usually find what I'm looking for slightly over half the time - if you don't remember key words/phrases, it can be tough.
