Grahame Grieve (Oct 22 2018 at 22:35):

With the release of MED-RT, Apelon asserts in their documentation that a Reference Terminology: is defined by Mayo as "a terminology in which concepts have a formal, machine-usable definition supporting data aggregation and retrieval."

My colleagues at Mayo are unable to claim attribution to this definition, albeit it does appear as a 'Reference Definition' on a CIMI wiki page hosted by Mayo.

A Google search reveals the definition of a Reference Terminology is found in the Glossary of a book you co-author. Unfortunately, I do not own the book and cannot tell if the term evolved elsewhere.

Who authored the definition of Reference Terminology?

Astrid Corinna Wolff (Oct 23 2018 at 08:40):

Does this help?

'From SNOMED CT Glossary'
https://confluence.ihtsdotools.org/display/DOCGLOSS/reference+terminology

_reference terminology_
_A terminology in which each term has a formal computer processable definition that supports meaning based retrieval and aggregation. SNOMED CT is a reference terminology_

Robert McClure (Oct 23 2018 at 17:31):

SNOMED-RT was the first health care "reference terminology." When they added in the Read Terms, they change the name to SNOMED CT. NDF-RT, and subsequently MED-RT, came after and used the same thinking SNOMED did, so I'd take the SNOMED CT Glossary as sufficient for sourcing the meaning.
