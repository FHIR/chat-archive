Hey! Took the fundamentals course as initial introduction to FHIR concepts and then proceeded to take the practice test in which I failed pretty badly. Followed up with the HL7 FHIR exam preparation course and having studied the concepts way more with practice quizzes given took the practice test again in which I did way better on and then passed the #proficiencyexam. Good way to take it all in and really understand it.

Sadhana Alangar (Jan 03 2022 at 18:29):

Pradeep,

Derek Buchanan who has taken the exam has a blog post: https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/fhir-certification-preparation-clinicians-also-derek-buchanan/?trackingId=HS22SqCwS3iuPDjOI8C%2BCQ%3D%3D. The FHIR Tip sheet offers some tips and resources for the exam: http://www.hl7.org/documentcenter/public/training/HL7%20FHIR%20Proficiency%20Study%20Guide.pdf. We do offer formal training: https://www.hl7.org/training/fhir-prep.cfm?ref=nav, and https://www.pathlms.com/hl7/product_bundles/1284. I hope that answers your question.
