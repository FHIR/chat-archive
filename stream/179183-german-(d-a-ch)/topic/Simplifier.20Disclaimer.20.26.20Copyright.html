Ward Weistra (Apr 24 2020 at 09:09):

Hi Peter! Just to answer from the Simplifier side: by uploading the content to Simplifier, you are only granting us some rights to fulfill our functions (found under 'Uploading Content' in our terms), like to save and host it. If you make the content public you also grant us and others the rights to download, use and link it. The copyright remains with the author.

More general I think it is a good practice to indeed add a license to your project under which conditions users can use and modify it, just like with a GitHub repository. An example can be seen on the page of this Nictiz project: https://simplifier.net/nictizstu3-zib2017.

I'm not familiar with the need for a Disclaimer, perhaps someone else is.

Alexander Zautke (Apr 24 2020 at 09:20):

Hi @Peter Osburg Generell unterliegt die FHIR-Spezifikation der Creative Commons C0 Lizenz. Siehe https://www.hl7.org/fhir/license.html#license. So "freizügig" muss man nicht werden, falls man dies aus welchen Gründen auch immer nicht möchte. Die meisten offiziellen HL7 Deutschland Projekte haben den folgenden Disclaimer enthalten:
