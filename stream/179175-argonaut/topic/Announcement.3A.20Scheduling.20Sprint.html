Eric Haas (Aug 01 2017 at 16:49):

he Argonaut Project is launching the a Scheduling Sprint to test implementation of the Appointment Availability Operation.

The Argonaut Scheduling Sprint is open to all. The testing and implementation program makes available testing resources, a reference implementation, a testing community, and access to subject matter experts to facilitate accelerated development and adoption of the FHIR scheduling artifacts.

You can find a detailed description of the Appointment Availability Sprint and overview of Argonaut Scheduling Project implementation program wiki: https://github.com/argonautproject/scheduling/wiki/Appointment-Availability-Sprint and the Argonaut Scheduling artifacts in the Implemenation Guide: http://build.fhir.org/ig/argonautproject/scheduling/OperationDefinition-appointment-find-sprint.html

Sprint 1 will run through Aug 23th. Please enroll for the sprint and record your testing results in the “results” tab of the Sprint 1 tracking sheet: https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/10kczyW4cTStPm1CuRAZVNb9WSgbAAZVoG3DNZuj2uyo/edit#gid=1742361329

We are planning a Sprint progress report on Wed Aug 9 at 3-4:00pm US Eastern and a debrief on Wed Aug 23 at 3-4:00pm US Eastern. You will receive an invitation for this call shortly.

For discussion and collaboration on either Argonaut-focused implementation activities or Argonaut-focused FHIR development please use:

the Argonaut Zulip Chat Stream (preferred): https://chat.fhir.org/#narrow/stream/argonaut
or the Argonaut Project Google Groups: https://groups.google.com/forum/?hl=en#!forum/argonaut-scheduling-workgroup
Feel free to contact me or @Brett Marquard any time if a you have any questions.
