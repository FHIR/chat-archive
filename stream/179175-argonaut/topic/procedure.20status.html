Jay Lyle (Jul 13 2018 at 12:15):

We have "cancelled" procedures, some of which are "aborted." Argonaut has aborted status but not cancelled. Assumption: for Argonauts, procedures cancelled before start just aren't procedures. True?

Michelle (Moseman) Miller (Jul 13 2018 at 13:11):

For our implementation, we are exposing the 'procedure history' or 'procedure statements' such that we don't have the use case for cancelled. In all transparency, all procedures we expose are 'completed' or 'entered-in-error'
For more information, see https://fhir.cerner.com/millennium/dstu2/general-clinical/procedure/

It is up to the user what procedures are clinically significant to document. Surgical and radiology procedures documented in other Cerner solutions will return if manually added by a clinician in the patient’s procedure history
