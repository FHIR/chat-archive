isaacvetter commented on Issue #119:

Hey @RicardoQuintano - I'm trying to address this issue, and am scratching my head, trying to figure out exactly what to do.

We've committed to updating the Launch Scenarios page to include a

"real life" example of session discovery

What does this mean to you? Would something like the below (albeit with some more content) meet our goal?

A radiologist working their EHR reporting system clicks a button to open their dictation system. The SMART dictation app performs the OAuth2 handshake to receive the hub.url and hub.topic. The app then subscribes to the radiologist's session by contacting the hub.url and supplying the hub.topic.

Github Notifications (FHIRcast) (Aug 21 2019 at 17:30):

RicardoQuintano commented on Issue #119:

Hi @isaacvetter,

The idea of the "real life example" is to support the reader to understand how FHIRcast can be used in a practical way. Your example is perfect and I would extend it (not only the session discovery part). Below is a different example.

"A physician checks radiology exams (such as TC-scan or X-rays) to support them to define the diagnosis and the care plan of their patients. The first time the physician needs to check the exams, they click on a button in the EHR to open the PACS image viewer app. The app performs the OAuth2 handshake to receive the hub.url and hub.topic. The app then subscribes to the physician's session by contacting the hub.url and supplying the hub.topic. Once the PACS image viewer app finishes the subscription process (subscription request and intent verification), every time the physician changes the patient in the EHR (change in context) the viewer will present the corresponding radiology exams from that specific patient."
