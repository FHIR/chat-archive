Topic: HPI Teams

Anne Goodwin (Jun 20 2019 at 03:42):

There is a desire to have a register of hospital clinical teams in the HPI. These are the teams typically led by a consultant with a variety of doctors and interns belonging to the team. There are 2 possible ways to model these teams 1. using the Organisation resource for the Team id and name and the PractitionerRole resource to record the participants belonging to the team or 2. using the CareTeam resource without a subject or encounter (care teams relating to a particular patient are out of scope for the HPI). Anyone have any guidance on pros and cons of each approach?

Lloyd McKenzie (Jun 20 2019 at 03:46):

CareTeam allows you to identify specific functions within the team. Organization does not. Organization is probably more widely supported by implementers and is available for use as an 'actor' in more places than CareTeam is in R4 (though CareTeam should appear everwhere relevant in R5)

Anne Goodwin (Jun 23 2019 at 22:12):

Do you mean that the CareTeam Participant Role would describe the specific function someone has on the team. I thought we could achieve this using the Organisation/PractitionerRole approach, by using the PractitionerRole.code to describe the function someone has in a team.

Lloyd McKenzie (Jun 23 2019 at 23:22):

PractitionerRole is describes professional capacity (e.g. "Registered Nurse"). CareTeam allows you to identify a specific function (e.g. "weekday homecare nurse"). The Role is independent of the team. The function is team- specific.

Anne Goodwin (Jun 23 2019 at 23:52):

OK Thanks

David Hay (Jul 01 2019 at 19:35):

Also, a 'sub-organization' can only be 'partOf' a single 'parent' organization. Is this always going to be the case for a careteam?

Peter Jordan (Jul 01 2019 at 22:24):

My view is that there are more fundamental building blocks for the NZ HPI Project to consider before moving onto Hospital Care Teams. In particular, the NZ Sector requires a directory that links providers to facilities and organisations - and a mechanism for keeping this up-to-date. IMHO, only when this is in place should we be looking at the more granular layer of care team registers within organisations and facilities; otherwise we are attempting to build the roof of a house before the walls have been completed.
