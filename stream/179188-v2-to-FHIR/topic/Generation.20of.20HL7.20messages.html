René Spronk (Feb 22 2019 at 07:54):

Largely for historic and contractual reasons the v2 access database isn't a free for use product, not for members or anyone else. I did request the HL7 BoD to free it up a couple of years ago, but to no avail, v2 was/is still dealt with differently than FHIR when it comes to this. Computable specifications of the other standards are readily available, although not always in an easy to use format.
If it's just about the bare bones v2 definitions you could have a look at some of the open source HL7 v2 tools out there, they also contain the v2 message definitions - however, you may be infringing on their work, and it won't be the official source of truth.

Grahame Grieve (Feb 22 2019 at 20:21):

HL7 standards and related artefacts are licensed to individuals for free use, but there is no license approval for those individuals to share any derived artefacts with each other. Most of the open source libraries are in violation of this license, and I don't know how that tension will be resolved.

This doesn't apply to FHIR artefacts, which have always and only been licensed under Creative Commons Domain (the derived software is mostly licensed under BSD or Apache)
