Josh Mandel (Oct 11 2021 at 01:13):

Is Common's 'SMART Health Card Verifier App' a separate app dedicated to verifiers? If so why is it not seen in the google play store, I only see the former app?

There's a SMART Health Card Verifier app (on Android and iOS; see https://thecommonsproject.org/smart-health-card-verifier) for verifying SHCs; this is distinct from the Common Health app which is for storing/managing health data including SHCs (analogous to Apple's Health app on iOS). I'm not sure what you mean by "only see the former".

Josh Mandel (Oct 11 2021 at 01:14):

What I am trying to work out is if we currently, or very soon, will have the ability for both iPhone and Android users to manually scan a SMART Health Card QR Code to allow them to store it on their phone, without the use of SMART on FHIR and a FHIR API.

You definitely never need a FHIR API to access a SMART Health Card. Even without any special wallet app, you can just save it as a file or print it out. (FHIR API access can be a convenient way to access a SHC if you already have an app connected to your FHIR API, but by design this is definitely in the "nice to have, not required" category.)

James Kizer (Oct 15 2021 at 19:46):

@Josh Mandel @Angus Millar sorry for missing this earlier in the week. CommonHealth supports the following methods of importing SHCs:

  • Via SMART on FHIR for data sources that support it
  • Scanning a QR code via the camera from within the app
  • Importing an image or PDF that has a QR code in it
  • Importing a .smart-health-card file
