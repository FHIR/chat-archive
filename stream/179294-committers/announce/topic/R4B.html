Lloyd McKenzie (Feb 10 2021 at 23:04):

The FMG reviewed the commits made to date against the R4B branch thus far and discovered that no one has actually submitted any content yet. In order to hit our Feb. 19 publication date, FMG would have to review the final content on Feb. 17th, which means that work groups would have to complete their QA and sign-off their content by Feb. 16 - 6 days from now. That doesn't seem realistic. There are also QA issues around the build including examples that updating and some questions around the inclusion of the CodeableReference data type. Based on that, the FMG is adjusting the R4B cycle as follows:

EOD Tuesday March 2nd - all work groups having changes for inclusion in R4B will have their changes made, QAed and minuted sign-off by the work group that the content is ready for ballot.
March 3rd - FMG will review the publication for quality and will sign off to proceed to ballot
March 5-April 4 - ballot will run

As a reminder, ballot scope is limited to:

  • changes and net new resources in the medication regulation and EBM space
  • new extensions
  • adjusting the targets of references and/or canonicals
  • technical corrections that affect tooling or are critical to navigation

Everything except the first one should be represented in a Jira tracker item with the "R4B" grouping

Please start migrating your changes into the branch ASAP so that if there are any issues or coordination that needs to happen, we figure that out sooner rather than later.

Discussion on the #committers stream please.
